# Spatio-Temporal Traffic Forecasting Datasets & Critical Analysis

Due to GitHub's strict 100MB file size limit, large spatio-temporal traffic datasets cannot be hosted directly in this repository. Below is a comprehensive directory of the canonical benchmark datasets relevant for Graph Neural Network (GNN) traffic forecasting, along with their official academic sources and direct download links. 

## ⚠️ Critical Analysis: The "Sensor Illusion"
When evaluating these datasets, it is vital to understand a foundational flaw in modern traffic forecasting research: **The Sensor Illusion**. 

Most benchmark datasets (like METR-LA and PeMS) are generated by fixed "loop detectors" buried only on major freeways. They completely ignore 85%+ of the physical road network (city streets, minor roads, intersections). When researchers claim their GNN captures "road network spatial dependencies" on these datasets, they are often incorrect. They are merely capturing the localized time-series correlations of a sparse sensor array. 

The datasets below are categorized by whether they suffer from this illusion, or if they capture true dense spatial coverage.

---

## 1. Datasets Subject to the "Sensor Illusion" (Loop Detectors)
These datasets are the primary benchmarks used to evaluate models like STGCN, DCRNN, Graph WaveNet, and our **TTM+GCN Adapter**. While excellent for testing time-series and sensor correlations, they *do not* represent a true, dense urban road grid.

### A. Traffic Speed Datasets
*   **METR-LA (Los Angeles)**
    *   **Description:** Speed readings from 207 loop detectors in Los Angeles County over 4 months (March–June 2012).
    *   **Download:** [DCRNN Official Google Drive](https://github.com/liyaguang/DCRNN) | [LibCity Mirror](https://github.com/LibCity/Bigscity-LibCity-Datasets)

*   **PEMS-BAY (San Francisco Bay Area)**
    *   **Description:** Speed data from 325 sensors in the San Francisco Bay Area over 6 months in 2017. 
    *   **Download:** [DCRNN Official Google Drive](https://github.com/liyaguang/DCRNN) | [LibCity Mirror](https://github.com/LibCity/Bigscity-LibCity-Datasets)

*   **Seattle Freeway Dataset**
    *   **Description:** Traffic speed data from 323 loop detectors in Seattle over a one-month period.
    *   **Download:** [Transdim Repository](https://github.com/xinyuanchen/transdim)

### B. Traffic Flow / Volume Datasets
*   **PeMSD4 (San Francisco Bay Area)** & **PeMSD8 (San Bernardino)**
    *   **Description:** Traffic flow data from 307 and 170 sensors respectively.
    *   **Download:** [ASTGCN Official Repository](https://github.com/Davidham3/ASTGCN)

*   **PeMSD7 (California)**
    *   **Download:** [STGCN Official Repository](https://github.com/VeritasYin/STGCN_IJCAI-18)

*   **EXPY-TKY (Tokyo Expressway)**
    *   **Download:** [LibCity Mirror](https://github.com/LibCity/Bigscity-LibCity-Datasets)

### C. Large-Scale Foundation Model Datasets
These modern datasets are massively larger, designed to test the scalability of models and zero-shot foundation models (like TTM). However, they still rely on sparse highway loop detectors.
*   **LargeST Benchmark**
    *   **Description:** A massive repository containing data from **8,600 sensors** covering a 5-year period in California. Specifically built to overwhelm traditional models.
    *   **Download:** [LargeST Official GitHub](https://github.com/liuxu77/LargeST)
*   **Raw Caltrans PeMS Data**
    *   **Download:** [Caltrans Performance Measurement System Data Portal](https://pems.dot.ca.gov/) (Requires free registration)

## 2. Datasets with True Spatial Coverage (Probe Vehicles)
These datasets **escape the Sensor Illusion**. Because they use GPS pings from thousands of moving vehicles (taxis, ride-shares), they capture traffic on highways, side streets, back alleys, and downtown grids. They naturally cover 60-80% of the entire physical road network, making them the ultimate test for true Spatial Graph models.

*   **NYC Taxi Dataset**
    *   **Description:** Records pickup/drop-off locations across Manhattan zones, used for true spatial trajectory forecasting across a dense city grid.
    *   **Download:** [NYC OpenData](https://opendata.cityofnewyork.us/data/)

*   **T-Drive Trajectory Data**
    *   **Description:** GPS trajectories of over 10,000 taxis traversing the dense urban grid of Beijing.
    *   **Download:** [Microsoft Research T-Drive](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/t-drive-trajectory-data-sample/)
